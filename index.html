What to look for

What makes a therapist bipolar-informed?

Plenty of therapists list "mood disorders" as a specialty. Fewer have the specific training, supervision, and case volume that bipolar-informed care requires. The difference shows up when symptoms don't fit a textbook: mixed episodes, rapid cycling, postpartum triggers, treatment resistance.

Bipolar-informed therapists tend to work in a few recognizable ways. They coordinate with psychiatrists for medication management. They know evidence-based modalities like Interpersonal and Social Rhythm Therapy (IPSRT) and Family-Focused Therapy. They help you spot early warning signs of a mood shift before it becomes an episode. They understand that stability is the long game, not a quick symptom fix.

Every profile here surfaces the operational details that actually matter: years treating bipolar specifically, modalities, coordination practices, insurance accepted, estimated wait time. No generic "everything" profiles.

Common questions

Questions we hear most

How is this different from Psychology Today?

Psychology Today is a paid listing where any therapist can claim any specialty. We review every therapist for documented bipolar work before they appear, so generalists claiming everything don't get in. Then the therapist maintains their own profile with the specifics that matter: training, years treating bipolar, modalities, insurance.

Is this free for patients?

Yes, always. Patients pay nothing to search, match, or contact therapists.

Do I need insurance?

Not required. Therapists list which insurance plans they accept and whether they offer sliding-scale rates or self-pay. Filter by what applies to you.

What if I'm not in California?

We're California-only for now. Building a directory right means starting narrow and expanding deliberately. Email us and we'll let you know when we launch in your state.

How are therapists vetted?

Every profile is license-verified against the California Department of Consumer Affairs. Bipolar expertise is confirmed through the therapist's listed training, bipolar-specific years of experience, treatment modalities, and operational details they personally maintain through a therapist portal.
