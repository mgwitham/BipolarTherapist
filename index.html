What to look for

What makes a therapist bipolar-informed?

Plenty of therapists list "mood disorders" as a specialty. Fewer have the specific training, supervision, and case volume that bipolar-informed care requires.

The difference shows up when symptoms don't fit a textbook: mixed episodes, rapid cycling, postpartum triggers, treatment resistance.

  • Coordinates with your psychiatrist for medication management
  • Knows evidence-based modalities like IPSRT and Family-Focused Therapy
  • Helps you spot early warning signs before an episode develops
  • Understands stability as the long game, not a quick symptom fix
