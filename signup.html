🏥 Practice Details

Keep this concrete and calm. Strong labels usually describe the first step, not a marketing slogan.

This is where people decide whether reaching out feels manageable. Clarify response time, what to include, and anything that helps avoid back-and-forth.

Strong example: New clients usually hear back within one business day. Please include your state, whether you are looking for therapy or medication support, and any insurance questions so we can point you to the right next step.

Reduce uncertainty. A strong answer helps someone picture the first contact, timeline, and what you are evaluating for fit.

Strong example: I usually begin with a 15-minute consultation to understand your goals, urgency, and whether therapy, psychiatry, or coordinated care makes the most sense before we schedule a full intake.

📝 About Your Practice

Focus on credibility and relevance: your training, who you help, and why bipolar clients or families may trust your care.

The strongest answers combine who you help, how you work, and what makes your bipolar-specific care distinct in practice.

Strong example: I work with adults living with Bipolar I and II using CBT, psychoeducation, and relapse-prevention planning. I help clients track sleep and mood patterns, strengthen routines, coordinate with psychiatry when needed, and build early-warning plans for mood shifts.
