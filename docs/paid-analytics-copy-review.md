# Paid Analytics Copy Review

| Area                        | Before                                    | After                                                                                              | Why it changed                                                                                                |
| --------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Page headline               | Activity                                  | Your listing performance this week                                                                 | Makes the surface feel like a weekly decision dashboard instead of a passive metrics card.                    |
| Support line                | Loading your profile activity...          | Loading patient discovery, contact paths, and next-step guidance...                                | Sets expectation that the dashboard explains behavior and actions, not just counts.                           |
| Top-value summary           | None                                      | Most important takeaway with performance + profile bottleneck framing                              | Gives therapists one fast answer to "what should I know?" and ties performance to listing quality.            |
| Profile completeness labels | None                                      | Profile strength, Listing readiness, Signals present, Highest-impact profile update                | Makes profile quality central and clearly shows what is strong, missing, and worth updating next.             |
| Action plan copy            | Keep booking link prominent.              | Action 1 / Action 2 / Watch next week with direct profile-update actions and expected benefit      | Turns recommendations into a ranked weekly action plan tied to visible evidence and concrete edits.           |
| Source interpretation       | How patients found you this week          | How patients found you, ranked by volume, plus top-source meaning                                  | Turns source bars into a decision signal by identifying the leading source and what that pattern suggests.    |
| Contact-path interpretation | How patients tried to reach you this week | How patients tried to reach you, ranked by volume, plus top-contact-path meaning                   | Focuses on contact intent rather than traffic alone and helps therapists protect the strongest path.          |
| What to watch next week     | Generic "watch next week" recommendation  | Separate What to watch next week module tied to contact intent rate and profile changes            | Gives the dashboard a forward weekly habit loop instead of ending with passive reporting.                     |
| Recency/data confidence     | Week label and updated date only          | Week label, updated date, low-signal/directional/decision-ready confidence copy                    | Prevents overconfident interpretation when volume is low and makes data quality more trustworthy.             |
| Preview note copy           | Dashboard only                            | Preview mode uses sample data. Live mode should focus on actual profile strength and next actions. | Keeps preview explanation compact and secondary while reserving prime space for therapist value in live mode. |

## Friction Audit

- More worth paying for: the dashboard now combines performance, profile strength, contact behavior, and concrete profile updates in one weekly workflow.
- More actionable: therapists can see the highest-impact profile gap, click straight into profile editing, and understand what to monitor next Monday.
- More helpful for profile strength: readiness signals now explain how booking link, availability, fee clarity, bipolar-fit language, and recent updates affect performance.
- Intentionally conservative: no ROI claims, no invented business outcome score, and low-signal weeks still carry caution language.
- Assumptions: therapists check weekly, contact clicks are more meaningful than views, profile quality affects match and contact behavior, and small samples should be treated as directional rather than definitive.
