# BipolarTherapyHub MVP Boundary

## Public MVP

The first live version should feel calm, trustworthy, and decision-ready.

Include:

- homepage with clear trust and matching explanation
- directory with strong filters, sorting, editorial lanes, and shortlist save
- therapist profile pages with trust signals, practical details, and next-step clarity
- guided match flow with shortlist, compare, first-contact recommendation, outreach drafts, and fallback planning
- therapist signup flow with strong profile-quality guidance

## Keep Internal Or Soft-Launched

These are valuable, but they should stay internal, hidden, or lightly exposed until operations are ready:

- internal match insights panel
- admin funnel analytics and strategy diagnostics
- concierge queue operations
- local-only feedback instrumentation surfaces
- any copy that frames the public experience as a prototype

## Product Standard For Launch

Public-facing pages should:

- explain what the product knows well
- explain what still needs human verification
- avoid exposing internal labels, internal metrics, or experimental language
- make the next step feel clear without pretending the system is omniscient

## Launch Narrative

BipolarTherapyHub is not just a directory.

It is a guided bipolar-specialist discovery and decision product that helps people:

- find stronger-fit therapists faster
- understand why a profile may fit
- compare practical tradeoffs
- take a calmer, clearer next step

## Immediate Boundary Decisions

- `match.html` should be treated as public-facing, not prototype-facing
- internal match insight panels should only appear in internal mode
- admin remains the operating system, not part of the public product
- public trust language should stay honest about profile data limits, responsiveness limits, and fit uncertainty

## Next Live-Launch Priorities

1. Final copy pass across homepage, directory, match, and profiles
2. Tighten therapist data quality on the highest-traffic profiles
3. Decide whether concierge is public-facing help copy or a hidden operational workflow
4. Add a basic production-safe analytics path when you are ready
5. Prepare domain/email cutover only when outreach and operations need it
