# Therapist Acquisition Playbook

## Goal

Get the first 10 to 25 launch-worthy therapist profiles for the `Los Angeles + California telehealth` wedge.

This is not a volume game yet.

The goal is:

- a small set of strong profiles
- credible bipolar relevance
- clear contact and trust details
- enough density that a shortlist feels useful

## The Right First Supply Mix

Aim for:

- 4 to 8 psychiatry / medication-management profiles
- 6 to 15 therapy-first profiles
- at least 3 profiles with family / couples support
- at least 3 profiles with culturally responsive care
- most profiles licensed for California telehealth
- several profiles physically based in Los Angeles or nearby areas

## Priority Geography

Start here:

1. Los Angeles
2. Pasadena / Glendale / Burbank
3. Santa Monica / Westside
4. Long Beach
5. San Diego and San Francisco only if they materially improve California telehealth coverage

The rule:

- local density first
- statewide telehealth second
- broader California only when it improves match usefulness

## What Counts As Launch-Worthy

A launch-worthy profile should have:

- bipolar-relevant focus that feels specific, not generic
- license details
- clear city and state
- whether telehealth is offered in California
- practical contact path
- first-step expectation
- care approach written in plain language
- fee and/or insurance clarity
- realistic availability signal

If a profile lacks several of these, it is not launch-ready yet.

## Who To Target First

Best first targets:

- psychiatrists who explicitly mention bipolar disorder, mood disorders, or medication management
- therapists who mention Bipolar I, Bipolar II, IPSRT, DBT, psychoeducation, relapse planning, family work, or serious mood disorder experience
- therapists who describe structured work with routines, sleep, medication coordination, caregivers, or relapse prevention

Lower-priority targets:

- generic anxiety/depression therapists with no bipolar signal
- providers with thin websites and no clear specialty language
- listings that make fit impossible to assess

## Where To Source Them

Use manual curation first.

Look in:

- therapist practice websites
- psychology / psychiatry group practices
- California telehealth practices
- bipolar-relevant clinician directories
- provider bios that explicitly mention bipolar disorder, mood disorders, psychosis, family support, or medication management

Do not import providers just because they are local.

Import them because they help the wedge feel stronger.

## Curation Rubric

Score each candidate from `0` to `2` on each dimension:

- bipolar relevance
- trust detail quality
- practical access clarity
- outreach clarity
- launch fit for LA + California telehealth

Meaning:

- `0` = weak
- `1` = usable
- `2` = strong

Prioritize profiles with totals of `7+`.

## Manual Workflow

Use this sequence:

1. Find candidate.
2. Read the provider page carefully.
3. Ask: would this actually help someone looking for bipolar-specific care?
4. If yes, draft the profile in structured form.
5. Fill the missing fields as far as public information reasonably allows.
6. Mark what still needs direct confirmation.
7. Only promote to launch-visible supply if the profile feels decision-ready.

## Data Quality Rules

Never overclaim.

Allowed:

- “focuses on Bipolar II”
- “offers medication management”
- “California telehealth listed”

Avoid:

- “best bipolar psychiatrist”
- “expert” unless the provider clearly claims it
- guessing insurance, availability, or treatment style

If a useful detail is unknown:

- leave it blank
- or frame it as something the user should confirm directly

## Trust-First Sourcing Order

Before polishing bios or extra profile texture, prioritize the fields that most affect trust and ranking:

1. license number
2. estimated wait time
3. insurance accepted or superbill reality
4. telehealth states
5. bipolar-specific years of experience
6. preferred contact path, contact guidance, and first-step expectation

These should be treated as sourcing priorities, not afterthoughts.

## Outreach Priority Order

If you start contacting therapists later, start here:

1. Providers whose public profile already looks strong and specific
2. Providers with California telehealth access
3. Providers with clear bipolar disorder language
4. Providers with clear intake or booking flows

Why:

- they are easiest to turn into believable launch inventory
- they are most likely to understand the value proposition quickly

## Early Outreach Message Shape

Keep it simple:

- explain the California-first wedge
- explain that the product is bipolar-specific, not generic
- explain that you care about decision-ready profile quality
- ask for missing practical details, not a huge partnership commitment

## Operating Standard

At this stage, quality beats scale.

A smaller California set that feels real is far better than a larger directory that feels random.
