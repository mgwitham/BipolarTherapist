# BipolarTherapyHub First Wedge

## The Job To Be Done

Help someone who already knows bipolar-specific care matters get to a short, credible list of therapists or psychiatrists faster than they could with a generic directory.

This is not the first wedge:

- everyone looking for any therapist
- broad mental-health search
- a giant national marketplace

This is the first wedge:

- people who already suspect they need bipolar-specific care
- people frustrated by generic therapist directories
- caregivers or partners helping someone find more specialized support
- users who want a calmer path from browsing to first outreach

## Who The First User Is

The first live user is probably one of these:

1. Someone diagnosed with Bipolar I or Bipolar II who has already had at least one poor-fit therapy experience.
2. A parent, partner, or caregiver trying to help someone find more specialized care.
3. Someone deciding between therapy-first support and psychiatry / medication-management support.

These users do not need infinite marketplace breadth.
They need:

- faster narrowing
- more confidence in specialization
- clearer trust signals
- a more practical first contact

## The First Supply Strategy

Do not try to launch with hundreds of mediocre profiles.

The first supply should be:

- 10 to 25 strong bipolar-relevant profiles
- concentrated in a few states, not everywhere
- a mix of therapy and psychiatry
- strong enough that a shortlist feels genuinely useful

Best early supply mix:

- 4 to 8 psychiatry / medication-management profiles
- 6 to 15 therapy-first profiles
- at least a few profiles with family / couples support
- at least a few profiles with culturally responsive care

## Geographic Strategy

Do not optimize for “national” first.

Pick a narrow footprint with enough density to feel real.

Recommended first launch shape:

- Los Angeles as the core density market
- California telehealth as the broader access layer

Why:

- Los Angeles is large enough to feel meaningful on its own
- California telehealth extends usefulness without pretending to be national
- the product can feel curated and dense instead of broad and thin
- this creates a cleaner brand story than a scattered multi-state launch

## Product Story For The First Launch

The story is not:

- “the smartest therapy algorithm”
- “therapy Tinder”
- “the biggest directory”

The story is:

BipolarTherapyHub helps you find a more credible bipolar-specialist next step without starting from scratch.

Public-facing message should consistently say:

- clearer bipolar-specific fit
- clearer trust and access details
- calmer outreach and shortlist decisions

## What To Measure First

Before monetization, measure whether the product helps people move.

Primary signals:

- shortlist saves
- match completions
- profile views from shortlist
- outreach starts
- whether users use backup paths after friction

Supply-side signals:

- number of profiles that feel truly decision-ready
- number of profiles with full trust and outreach details
- number of profiles that feel launchable without apology

## What Not To Do Yet

Do not do these next:

- payments
- broad SEO expansion
- aggressive growth loops
- lots more matching complexity
- mass therapist acquisition without a quality bar

The current constraint is not product imagination.
It is believable supply and real-world usefulness.

## The Right Manual Loop

For the first real-world learning cycle:

1. Curate 10 to 25 strong profiles.
2. Walk through the full user journey on those profiles.
3. Ask: could a real person confidently choose one of these therapists?
4. If not, improve the data and trust surfaces before adding more features.

## Narrow Launch Recommendation

If I were launching this from first principles, I would launch as:

“A curated bipolar-specialist shortlist and matching tool for Los Angeles and California telehealth, starting with a small set of decision-ready profiles.”

That is smaller than a marketplace story, but much stronger.

## Next Task

The next best concrete task is:

Upgrade the seeded supply and directory defaults to reflect a real first wedge.

That means:

- rebalance the visible therapist mix toward a realistic first-market footprint
- concentrate the visible footprint around Los Angeles plus California telehealth
- make the strongest 10 to 25 profiles feel consistent and decision-ready
- tighten homepage and directory copy around the narrow launch story
- stop implying breadth that the first version does not truly have
